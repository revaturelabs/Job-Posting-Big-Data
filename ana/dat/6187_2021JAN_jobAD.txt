 Deputy Manager 
 An exciting new job opportunity has arisen for a committed Deputy Manager to work in an exceptional nursing home based in the Shipley, West Yorkshire area. 
 As the Deputy Manager you will be working for one of UK's leading healthcare providers 
 This nursing home is a home for young adults with disabilities. 
 It cares for people who have a variety of specialist needs including amputees, people with brain injuries Multiple Sclerosis (MS), Motor Neuron Disease, Huntington's Disease, Epilepsy and many other complex conditions 
 As the Deputy Manager your key responsiblities include: 
 
 
     Responsible for all aspects of running the home in the absence of the Care Home Manager, accountable for the standard of care delivered within their home
 
 
 
     Leading the clinical team on a shift by shift basis
 
 
 
     Directing support staff to ensure the delivery of quality care
 
 
 
     Working closely with the care & nursing team, relatives and other agencies to ensure person-centered care plans are in place that put the individual at the centre of all interactions, ensuring dignity and respect are maintained at all times
 
 
 
     Carry out duties in accordance with NMC guidelines, leading the care & nursing team on shift and providing clinical leadership, practice development, feedback and reflection to Registered Nurses and Care Assistants as well as new and developing members of staff
 
 
 
 
 An ideal Deputy Manager must have these skills and experience as they are benefits: 
 
 
     Must have a vast amount of experience in similar setting
 
 
 
     Have a working knowledge of the regulatory framework relating to Care Homes
 
 
 
     Possess good communication and interpersonal skills, both verbal and written
 
 
 
     Demonstrate the ability to lead, support and motivate a dedicated care team
 
 
 
 **The successful Deputy Manager will receive an excellent salary of £18.75 per hour and the annual salary for this position is £39,000 per annum. 
 This exciting position is a Full Time role working 40 hours a week. 
 In return for your hard work and commitment you will receive the following generous benefits:** 
 
 
     Excellent rates of pay
 
 
 
     28 days paid holiday
 
 
 
     Smart uniforms
 
 
 
     Free CRB checks
 
 
 
     Four days induction
 
 
 
     NVQ training
 
 
 
 To be considered for this position you must be qualified as a Nurse either RGN, RMN or RNLD with a valid NMC Pin
