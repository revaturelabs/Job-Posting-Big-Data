 NPI Technician / Manufacturing Engineer 
 MRO Electronic Supply 
 __Calgary, AB 
 Apply Now 
 MRO Electronic Supply is an ISO 9001 certified custom cable assembly manufacturer specializing in over-molded cable assemblies for harsh environments and a distributor of industrial electronic components. We specialize in service to a number of industries, including Oil & Gas, Mining, Agriculture, Automotive, Telecom and Military. We take pride in our history and growth (established in 1991, currently 100+ employees), as well as our commitment to quality and customer service. 
 
 
 Why work at MRO? 
 
 
 Job security in a growing company 
 
 
 Opportunity for advancement 
 
 
 Great culture - our employees enjoy a positive work-life balance while thriving in an environment that is ever-changing and motivated by new and exciting challenges 
 
 
 We offer competitive compensation & benefits package with health spending account & EFAP program 
 
 
 Do you find great joy in being a team player who likes to have a leading role in the introduction of new products and solving challenging technical issues in a manufacturing environment, then read on! 
 The key mission of this job is the introduction of new products (Custom Cables & Harnesses) into volume production by defining requirements, reviewing designs and driving technical solutions for problems encountered during the proto/pilot phase of the product. 
 Essential Job Functions: 
 
 
 Act as link between Suppliers, Engineering and Manufacturing team when new products are released 
 
 
 Support the team during new design reviews by representing manufacturing and suppliers 
 
 
 Diagnostic troubleshooting, rework/repair of non-conforming manufactured products and production equipment 
 
 
 Perform revision updates, rework or repair per approved disposition using a variety of tools and soldering equipment 
 
 
 Prepare and manage documentation of parts and products 
 
 
 Comply with policies, guidelines and regulatory requirements per the Quality System 
 
 
 Maintain a clean and organized work area to facilitate manufacturing functions 
 
 
 Report problems with quality, processes, equipment and materials to Production Supervisor 
 
 
 Assemble and test product and various electro-mechanical production equipment 
 
 
 Effectively communicating with diverse group of people; demonstrating comfort navigating language barriers 
 
 
 Specific vision requirements include close vision and color vision 
 
 
 Education Requirements 
 
 Post-secondary and/or technical school education is highly desirable with a minimum of one year of experience with electrical and/or electronic disciplines. 
 
 Qualification Requirements: 
 
 
 Intermediate to advanced computer skills 
 
 
 Electrical/electronics technical knowledge and experience - 1 year 
 
 
 Ability to read and interpret technical drawings, schematics and specs 
 
 
 Good balance of theoretical and practical experience and "hands on" in a manufacturing and production environment 
 
 
 Experience using test equipment 
 
 
 Soldering experience considered a strong asset 
 
 
 Able to work independently with limited supervision 
 
 
 Great time management skills 
 
 
 Ability to work under pressure and to meet deadlines 
 
 
 **Other Desired Skills & Characteristics: ** 
 
 
 Excellent attention to detail 
 
 
 Good communication skills – written & verbal 
 
 
 Ability to define problems, collect data, establish facts, and draw conclusions based on experience, research and input from peers 
 
 
 Ability to maintain focus and multitask effectively 
 
 
 Hours:

 Job Type: Full-time
