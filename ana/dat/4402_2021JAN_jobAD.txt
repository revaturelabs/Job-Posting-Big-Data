 Emergency Department Technician - Victoria Hospital 
 Victoria Hospital 
 __London, ON 
 Apply Now 
 Posting Period 
 Open:  November 18, 2020 
 Deadline:  November 24, 2020 
 UNIFOR 
 This position is covered by a collective agreement. 
 Department Name 
 Emergency Department - Victoria Hospital 
 The Emergency Department Technician works as a member of the clinical team and supports patient care activities in the Emergency Department in conjunction with physicians, nurses and other support staff for both medical and mental health patient populations. The Emergency Department Technician monitors, troubleshoots and provides basic equipment maintenance to specialty equipment in the Emergency Department, orders and stocks supplies, and provides on-going communication to facilitate daily operation of the department. 
 Regular Part Time 
 Rate of Pay:  $23.22 per hour - $24.57 per hour 
 Hours:  Regular Part Time 
 Qualifications 
Successful completion of a Paramedic Diploma from a community college 
 Current Basic Life Support for Healthcare Providers course:  BLS-HCP(C) to be renewed annually 
Valid First Aid certificate 
Demonstrated proficient knowledge of CPR and ability to perform compressions during CPR 
Ability to complete Management of Aggressive Behaviour training to be renewed annually 
Demonstrated ability to meet the physical demands of the position 
Demonstrated proficient Aseptic/Sterile Technique 
Demonstrated excellent communication skills (written and verbal) 
Able to demonstrate flexibility, adaptability to change, as well as organize, prioritize and manage time in a rapidly changing environment 
Advanced understanding and knowledge of emergency department equipment and related mechanical skills 
Ability to problem solve with demonstrated initiative, resourcefulness, and well developed decision making skills 
Demonstrated attention to detail 
Basic knowledge of legislation regarding Mental Health 
Demonstrated basic knowledge of medical terminology 
Demonstrated advanced competence with regard to patient safety 
Demonstrated ability to work effectively and cooperatively as a member of a team 
Demonstrated advanced competence with regard to being patient focused with a service oriented attitude 
Demonstrated computer proficiency with Automated Clinical Systems 
Demonstrated practice and commitment to the principles of patient and family centered care 
Demonstrated practice and commitment to patient and staff safety at LHSC 
Demonstrated practice and commitment to LHSC’s Mission, Vision and Values 
Demonstrated ability to attend work on a regular basis 
 Immunization Requirements: 
Provide vaccination records or proof of immunity against measles, mumps, rubella and varicella (chicken pox), Hepatitis B, Tetanus/ Diphtheria/Polio; Meningitis. 
Provide documentation of the Tuberculosis skin testing (two step) 
 We are committed to providing a safe, healthy and inclusive work environment that inspires respect. LHSC encourages applications from persons with disabilities and we are committed to providing accommodations upon request. 
 Your interest in this opportunity is appreciated. Only those applicants selected for an interview will be contacted. Successful candidates, as a condition of job offer, would be required to provide a satisfactory Police Information Check (original document) completed in the last 3 months.
