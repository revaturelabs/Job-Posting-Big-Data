Mayo Clinic midwives are independent providers practicing at the top of their license within a multidisciplinary obstetrical care team focused on compassionate, expert, and personalized care. Mayo Clinic midwives enjoy high-level resource support in a collaborative, multi-specialty practice with access to specialists in maternal fetal medicine, medical genetics, reproductive medicine and other medical and surgical specialties as needed throughout the Mayo Clinic Enterprise. In an evidence-based practice where midwives are an integral part of the culture, you will have opportunities to pursue your professional passions in areas such as midwifery education, work as a faculty member, or the pursuit of research with strong institutional support.The vision for the Certified Nurse Midwives at Mayo Clinic is to provide the best midwifery care in the world by modeling evidence based midwifery in a team-based approach to care. Midwives at Mayo Clinic believe that every patient deserves a midwife as part of their care and strive to support their patients in a holistic, whole person way while working with the first class medical care that is available in the Mayo enterprise. Mayo Clinic midwives provide antepartum, intrapartum, and postpartum care to women delivering at Mayo Clinic’s flagship location in Rochester, Minnesota and support the midwifery teams at satellite locations within the Mayo Clinic Health System. Midwives provide prenatal care through an innovative OB nest model that combines telehealth, at home medical devices, hands-on provider care and connected care visits with an OB nurse to reduce on-site visits and expand access to prenatal services based on patient preference. As a unit, obstetrics team members assist in the delivery of more than 2,400 babies each year.Additional career opportunities include academic advancement, leadership of committees and teams, participation in quality improvement projects and research studies, and development of leading edge innovation. CNMs are encouraged to join hospital committees focused on strategic planning and clinical guideline development and management. Midwives also play an integral role in the clinical education of midwives, resident physicians, nurse practitioners, physician assistants, and medical students. CNMs are eligible for faculty status through the Mayo Clinic College of Medicine and Science and provide didactic and clinical teaching to learners throughout their educational program. Mayo Clinic midwives further the midwifery credential on a national scale through engagement with professional associations, advocacy, publication, and presentation at conferences. Pursue your passion and contribute to the future of midwifery at the #1 hospital in the United States.QualificationsMasters or Doctoral prepared graduate of an accredited school of nursing and midwifery program accredited by the American College of Nurse Midwives. Body mobility, coordinated dexterity of hands, ears, and eyes; sensory acuity, and verbal ability are required.Experience as a teacher or preceptor in an academic medical center, participation in quality improvement or research projects, and expertise in multidisciplinary team collaboration are desirable. Additional certifications in Obstetric Ultrasound, Advanced Fetal Monitoring and/or Lactation consultation are preferred.License or certificationEligible for RN and APRN licensure in Minnesota.
