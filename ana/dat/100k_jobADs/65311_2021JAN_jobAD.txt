"LabCorp announces plans to help get workers back on the jobSUBSCRIBE NOWAs low as $3 for 3 monthsSUBSCRIBE NOWAs low as $3 for 3 monthsNews Sports Entertainment Lifestyle Opinion USA TODAY Obituaries E-Edition LegalsSubscribeLabCorp announces plans to help get workers back on the jobTimes-NewsBurlington’s LabCorp announced a program Thursday to help employers test employees so they can get back to work.“Many U.S. companies have asked for LabCorp’s assistance as they implement their return-to-work strategies for the summer and fall,” LabCorp said in a press release.Those with active COVID-19 symptoms should contact their doctors or healthcare providers. For others, LabCorp will help identify employees with possible COVID-19 symptoms who are reporting to work or requesting to return to work.First, a questionnaire and noninvasive temperature check, managed on-site by trained staff, will screen for symptoms that could be COVID-19.After that, in accordance with employers’ policies and procedures, employees can be cleared for work or sent to employers’ human resources departments or other staff for further testing.Onsite, employees can collect nasal swabs themselves, with staff available to assist with collection and shipment. Offsite, the nasal swab test can be sent directly to employees’ homes.The nasal swab test hasn’t been cleared by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration, but has received temporary emergency-use authorization solely for detecting nucleic acid from SARS-CoV-2.Asymptomatic employees who think they might have had COVID-19 can be tested for antibodies. “Antibody testing should not be used as the sole basis to diagnose or exclude infection,” LabCorp said.Onsite, the antibody test can be a dried blood spot fingerstick test, or a blood draw. Offsite, blood is drawn at a LabCorp location or a Walgreens, with a phlebotomist collecting and preparing the sample.The antibody test uses the Euroimmun platform, also authorized for emergency use by the FDA. “LabCorp completed independent validation studies on this testing,” the company said.All test results will be available in two to four days.“We have been helping keep people safe and healthy for 50 years and are uniquely positioned to assist employers in their return-to-work strategies,” said Dr. Brian Caveney, chief medical officer and president of LabCorp Diagnostics.For more information, visit https://www.labcorp.com/coronavirus-disease-covid-19/employer-services.Careers Accessibility Site Map Legals Our Ethical Principles Terms of Service Privacy Policy Your California Privacy Rights / Privacy PolicyDo Not Sell My Info / Cookie PolicyContact Us Support Local Business Advertise Your Business Advertising Terms and Conditions Licensing & Reprints Legal Notices Help Center Subscriber Guide My Account My Print Account Give FeedbackSubscribe Today Newsletters Facebook Twitter E-EditionJobs Cars Homes Classifieds Pay Your Ad Bill Reviewed.com 10Best Reach Local© 2021 www.the-dispatch.com.
