Save 97%.News Sports Blue Raiders Lifestyles Opinion Obituaries E-Edition LegalsSubscribeJob Shadow: Chasing ghosts in Middle TennesseeHelen Comer and Michelle WillardThe Daily News JournalMURFREESBORO — Being a ghost hunter isn’t as exciting as television shows make it out to be.“It is not exciting or scary. We are in the dark or talking to air and nothingness,” said Beth Swain, leader of Shadow Chasers of Middle Tennessee.The group spends nights investigating reports of paranormal activity for homeowners and business owners in the area. In October, members raise funds for their efforts with ghost tours of downtown Murfreesboro.Jacob Skoropat, the administrative assistant for the Murfreesboro group, said the TV shows only portray the “glorious side of our job but most of the time you spend it talking to air and staring at equipment waiting for it to go off.”But when the equipment goes off, it's worth the wait, Swain said.She said “catching unexplained voices, shadows or apparitions on recorder and cameras” are her favorite parts of the job.Skoropat said he enjoys learning the histories surrounding the places they investigate is his favorite part.“You never know what you’ll find,” he said.If you go …Murfreesboro’s 10 Most HauntedWalking ghost tour of downtown Murfreesboro starting at Big B Cleaners.Tours leave out every 30 minutes from 7-9:30 p.m. Friday and Saturday.For more information, visit www.shadowchasersmidtn.org.WeeklyEvery Wednesday The Daily News Journal will feature a photo essay focusing on a job in Rutherford County.
