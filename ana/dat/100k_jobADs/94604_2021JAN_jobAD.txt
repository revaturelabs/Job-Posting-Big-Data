In fact, thanks to its increasing popularity, some common myths about computer systems as a career choice are only growing stronger.You’re likely familiar with the stereotype of a computer and information systems professional. While a few stereotypes may have some basis in reality, most of them are exaggerated or simply wrong. Let’s tackle some of the most common misconceptions about computer systems and technology as an industry, and finally put them to rest once and for all.MYTH: Being in the computer-related field means sitting alone at a computer all day.FACT: The reality is, within the computer and information systems field, there are few completely solitary positions. The majority of these roles are on small to medium-sized teams where regular collaboration is critical. The core of computer science is problem-solving, and you can’t solve every problem alone. More often than not, you’ll be interacting with colleagues, clients and customers on a daily basis.MYTH: You have to be nerdy to be successful in the technology field.FACT: The computer and information systems field is vastly diverse and is becoming more so as time progresses. While many careers within this industry involve a lot of math – probably where this stereotype comes from – there are a variety of ways to advance your skills in problem-solving depending on your ability levels.MYTH: Computer science is boring.FACT: While the phrase “computer science” might cause some people to glaze over, the field is rapidly growing in new and creative ways. As mentioned, computer science boils down to problem-solving, and many of the best solutions to complicated problems involve a great deal of creativity and out-of-the-box thinking.MYTH: Computer and information systems professionals are mostly men.FACT: Actually, the myth that a majority of computer and information systems professionals are men is true. However, recent efforts have encouraged women to pursue careers in science, technology, engineering and math – so there are a variety of opportunities available for women who are interested in the field. Today, technology professionals come from a wide range of backgrounds and have diverse interests.You don’t have to fit one rigid structure to be a successful computer science professional. The field itself is made up of a diverse group of individuals, and there are opportunities for specializations in countless fields.
