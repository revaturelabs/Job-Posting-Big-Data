Frank	You’re set on a new career, huh? Good for you! We have a tendency to get stuck in our ways sometimes, and breaking that dull routine is hard to do. You’ve already accomplished the first step: coming to alwaysbejobhunting.com for advice! Now, it’s time to think about where to go from here.Today, we’re going to look at what you need to know about training for a new career. While it’s nice to think that you can walk straight into something new, that’s probably not the case. You might need training or experience, or probably a combination of both. It’s time to break down a few things you need to know.What Do You Need?It might sound a bit obvious, but you need to be aware of the training and experience requirements your ideal career entails. Let’s assume you’re going to start at the bottom; what do you need in order to get there? Look online for resources to help you with this. You could even talk to a friend or family member that might work in the same field. Ask them how they got started, and if they know of any opportunities that might be going. Most people will be willing to help you, and it could be invaluable as you get started.Get Started As Soon As You CanMost of the time, you’re going to need to keep a full-time job while you get started in a new career. If you can’t land a job straight away, that doesn’t mean you shouldn’t take steps to help you get there. We’ve already mentioned that you might need training requirements to get that job, so what are you waiting for?! Let’s say you wanted to work in Phlebotomy. You can start training in this field right now, and you can learn more about Phlebotomy school cost on the web to help you out. Whatever field you’re looking to work in, volunteer, train and do whatever else it takes to get you started.Convey Your EnthusiasmLanding that first job in a new career is not easy. Even if you’ve managed to obtain the right amount of training and voluntary experience, there’s lots of competition. You’ve got no proof of your skills in a paid work environment, so some employers will be skeptical. The most important thing to do at that interview is convey your enthusiasm. You chose a new career for a reason, right?! If you love the idea of working in this field as much as you say you do, show it! Don’t panic too much about this, your passion will naturally shine through if you really enjoy what you’re doing.Starting a new career is exciting. We only live once, so we should do what makes us feel fulfilled and happy. None of these tips will sound laborious or dull if you’re truly interested in following a specific path. Remember that you can’t change everything overnight, so just take small steps to get where you want. Soon, you’ll have a fantastic job in your chosen career!This post has been contributed by Ryan Gatt, it contains affiliate links.Share this:ShareFacebookTwitterRedditEmailLinkedInPrintLike this:Like Loading...RelatedThis entry was posted in career switch, finding a first job and tagged career changes, new job, search tips.
