CCPL is committed to this support by providing free resources, services, information, and programs that assist the members of our community in developing their skills and meeting their professional needs.Workflow:PublishedWorkforce Services and ProgramsCCPL’s staff is providing services and programs related to workforce and professional development. These programs and services are currently being provided virtually on our website and social media pages on Facebook and YouTube as well as through email support and telephonically.Programs: CCPL librarians and staff are providing video programs on topics related to workforce development. Access those on our Facebook and YouTube pages.Services: CCPL librarians and staff are available to assist patrons with their workforce development needs. This includes but is not limited to:Resume assistanceAccessing unemployment resources and informationTechnology supportGuidance and information on professional development resourcesDuring our closure, patrons may access our staff to provide this assistance online via email, text or chat as well as telephonically. CCPL’s remote telephone service is available Monday through Friday from 10 a.m. to 7 p.m. To connect with CCPL, simply call 843-805-6930, leave a voice message, and a library staff member will return your call. Calls will not be answered live, but messages will be returned in a timely manner based on the order they are received. Response time may vary depending on call volume. Calls returned by CCPL staff will appear differently on caller ID than the number originally dialed.In addition to the phone service, you can also connect directly with a librarian on CCPL’s website via chat, text and email (Monday - Friday, 10 a.m. - 7 p.m.), or message CCPL on Facebook.Workflow:PublishedI was laid off from a full-time job due to COVID-19If your work is affected by COVID-19, you can apply for Unemployment Insurance from the SC Department of Employment and Workforce. Eligibility for benefits can only be determined after you have filed a claim for benefits.There are many companies hiring extra help due to the demand created by COVID-19. CCPL has put together a list of places to search for employment.CCPL offers an array of electronic resources, databases and subscription-based services, many of which are focused on workforce development. Those can be accessed using your library card number.Workflow:PublishedI am self-employed or a contract employeeExpress Bridge Loans allow small businesses who currently have a business relationship with an SBA Express Lender to access up to $25,000 quickly. These loans have to be repaid in part or in full.The Economic Injury Disaster Loan Advance will provide economic relief to businesses that are currently experiencing a temporary loss of revenue. Funds will be made available following a successful application. This loan advance will not have to be repaid.Lender Match is a free online referral tool that connects small business borrowers with participating SBA lenders. The online tool matches you with potential lenders by your industry type, age of business, location and financial need.Workflow:PublishedI am a gig worker or hospitality and tourism workerRWCF is working to collect information and links to resources that will help restaurants and workers deal most effectively with the COVID-19 Emergency. The page is continuously updated.The One Fair Wage is providing cash assistance to restaurant workers, car service drivers, delivery workers, personal service workers and more who need the money they aren’t getting to survive.THE SCRLA has put together a regularly updated page of resources for people who work in hospitality and the food and beverage industry in South Carolina.
