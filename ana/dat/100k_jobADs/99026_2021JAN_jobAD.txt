These lie scattered in the Arabian Sea about 280 km off Kerala coast between 8’ to 23’3” North Latitude and 71’ and 74’ East Longitude. Lakshadweep forms a single Indian district and is governed by an administrator appointed by the President of India under article 239 of the constitution. In Lakshadweep Coconut is the only major crop with high production, standing first in India on count of productivity per hectare. Lakshadweep coconut is branded is organic product. Coconut fibre extraction and conversion into fibre products is the main industry in the Islands. Under Government Sector, there are seven coir fibre factories, in which five are coir production-cum-distribution centres and seven fibre curling units, functioning under coir sector. These units produce produced coir fibre and coir yarn in addition to other coir products like curled fibre, corridor mats, mat and matting. A few coir twisting units also function in private sector. Lakshadweep comprises the only coral atolls of the country. There is an estimation of about one Lakh tonnes of tuna and tuna like fishes and about an equal quantity of shark in the sea around Lakshadweep as fishing serves as the main livelihood of the islanders.
