Academic Pediatric generalists and pediatric sub-specialists are welcome to respond.Position:The Vice Chair will lead Department efforts in recruitment, curriculum, CME and all other areas related to Pediatric Medical EducationThe Vice Chair will help develop and manage the Department's education budgetThe Vice Chair will report directly to the Department Chair of Pediatrics and play a key role as a member of the senior leadership teamThe Department of Pediatrics is composed of 123 full-time and more than 200 volunteer faculty members, organized into 16 pediatric medical subspecialty Divisions that partner with four key pediatric surgical subspecialty servicesAssociated Centers of Excellence include The Boggs Center for Developmental Disabilities, the Child Health Institute of NJ, and the Institute for the Study of Child DevelopmentThe Medical Campus is the home of the Rutgers Cancer Institute of NJ, the PPSE&G Children's Specialized Hospital, the Child Health Institute of New Jersey, and pediatric emergency and inpatient clinical services at the Bristol-Myers Squibb Children's Hospital of the Robert Wood Johnson University Hospital in New Brunswick, NJThe Department is undergoing a renaissance during the merger of two major health systems to form RWJ Barnabas. During the past three years, more than 40 new faculty members have been hired to expand the Department's academic and clinical programs. The Department's faculty recruitment efforts are ongoingCommunity:New Brunswick, NJ has been nicknamed the Health Care City as it is home to a number of leading healthcare institutionsMiddlesex County and nearby Somerset and Mercer Counties offer a broad diversity of living options from the sophisticated and manageable downtown of historic New Brunswick to charming towns that date to colonial days to suburban and rural settings.Rutgers University is one of nine US colleges that date to colonial times. The main campus of Rutgers University is in New Brunswick and nearby Piscataway. They offer residents and faculty an exciting university atmosphere and easy access to the many educational, sports, cultural and social events that a major Big 10 University offers.Outstanding schools, beautiful homes, and many recreational and social amenities can easily be found throughout the areaNew Brunswick provides all of this and is within one hour drive and easy rail access to both New York City and PhiladelphiaIf you desire a professionally rewarding academic leadership position where you can demonstrate your passion for advancing excellence and innovation in medical education, I highly recommend you consider this opportunity.Interested candidates please contact:Ken SammutVice President of Recruiting888-372-9415ksammut@crosscountry.comPlease reference Position ID 165074 when responding.ShareFacebookTwitterLinkedInApply for Department of Pediatrics Vice Chair for Medical Education - New JerseyAlready uploaded your CV?
