Save 90%.News Sports Packers Business Life Obituaries E-Edition LegalsSubscribeLil' Pets moves to Stevens PointNathan VineUSA TODAY NETWORK-WisconsinView CommentsLil’ Pets is marking its 25th year in a unique way, with a new home and a change to the business.Owners Bev Stoflet and Carla Van Tassel opened up Doggie Boutique Lil’ Pets at 23 Park Ridge Drive, across the street from Walgreens and Nice as New, in January. The business had previously been located at 2542 Post Road in Plover, but Stoflet said they needed a new home with the $36 million state project to widen Post Road scheduled to begin later this year.Since opening in 1990, the business has evolved from a full-fledged pet store to selling puppies and supplies while offering dog grooming. With the new move Stoflet said the business will operate as a boutique, though they will still sell a limited number of dogs from a single vendor whom Stoflet - who has raised dogs for over 50 years - has worked with for years. Van Tassel will also offer grooming services, with more than 30 years of experience.“When you come to a business like ours, you know you’re working with someone who knows you and your animal,” Stoflet said.Doggie Boutique Lil’ Pets offers a wide variety of dog supplies from toys, beds, leashes, and clothing. Stoflet said the store carries Wisconsin-based NutriLife and Fromm Family food along with all-natural treats made in the United States, and a selection of organic shampoos and cologne.Doggie Boutique Lil’ Pets is open from 9 a.m. to 6 p.m. Monday through Friday, and from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. on Saturday. For more information, please call 715-344-8085 or email lil-pets@sbcglobal.net.
