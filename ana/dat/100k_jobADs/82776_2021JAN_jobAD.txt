Key Duties and ResponsibilitiesThis post provides complete information on the job description of a real estate attorney, to help you learn the work they do.It highlights the key duties, tasks, and responsibilities that commonly make up the real estate attorney work description.It also presents the major requirements employers/recruiters will expect you to fulfill if you are seeking the real estate attorney role.Please, continue reading to increase your knowledge of the real estate attorney career:What Does a Real Estate Attorney Do?A real estate attorney is responsible for handling property transactions by dealing with legal issues such as mortgage, zoning, and transfers of titles and deeds.His/her job description entails overseeing real estate purchases and preparing contracts, while also carrying out all the job duties in a strict compliance with the current legislation.In other words, a real estate attorney can be described as a professional individual whose profession is to oversee the legal aspects of a real estate transaction by preparing and reviewing legal documents that are related to real estate, negotiating the terms and conditions of real estate transactions, and facilitating the transfer of titles.One of the major duties that are carried out by real estate attorneys is the handling of boundary disputes and the lease of agreement issues, which may involve going to court over option agreements and commercial space disputes.He/she may also be required to legal work for real estate agents, brokers, sellers, and buyers.A real estate attorney is also saddled with the responsibility of identifying and monitoring the risk in real estate documentation and giving advice to his/her clients accordingly.He/she is also expected and required to interpret laws, rulings, and regulations for real estate transactions, while also drafting and negotiating real estate transactions.It is also the duty of a real estate attorney to make sure that the necessary and appropriate approvals are in place before real estate transactions are executed.A real estate attorney must also ensure that all of his/her activities follow the applicable standards and protocols set out in laws.He/she must also offer legal advice on property management, zoning violations, limitations, and agreements on real estate, property taxes, and value estimates.To become a real estate attorney, some of the skills and qualities you will need to have include excellent verbal and written communication skills, strong networking skills, and excellent negotiation skills; good organizational skills, strong attention to detail, and self-motivation skills.Others are good analytical skills, computer proficiency, and familiarity with at least Microsoft Office Programs; and excellent time management skills.In terms of academic qualifications, the job role/position of a real estate attorney demands that the ideal candidate possesses a Bachelor’s degree in Law or in a related discipline.Real Estate Attorney Salary: The average salary for a real estate attorney according to Payscale is $77,824 annually.Real Estate Attorney Job Description Example/Sample/TemplateReal trespassing, injuries, while also defining limitations/boundaries and working towards resolving disputesGiving legal advice on property management, zoning violations, restrictions and agreements on real estate, property taxes, and value estimatesHandling regulatory and compliance related servicesDrafting and maintaining routine leases and amendments.Real Estate Attorney Job Description for
