Opportunity Announces Southwest Florida October 2020 Economic DataNov 20, 2020TALLAHASSEE, Fla. — Today, the Florida Department of Economic Opportunity announced the Fort Myers area private-sector employment decreased by 5,800 jobs (-2.5 percent) over the year. The Fort Myers area’s unemployment rate was 5.5 percent in October. The Naples area private-sector employment decreased by 4,200 jobs (-3.0 percent) over the year. The Naples area’s unemployment rate was 5.1 percent in October.The industry losing the most jobs over the year in the Fort Myers area was leisure and hospitality (-6,700 jobs). Two major industries gained jobs over the year in the Fort Myers area: professional and business services (+2,400 jobs); and construction (+2,200 jobs). The industries losing the most jobs over the year in the Naples area were: leisure and hospitality (-1,900 jobs); and trade, transportation, and utilities (-1,300 jobs). Two major industries gained jobs over the year in the Naples area: manufacturing (+500 jobs); and construction (+200 jobs).Statewide, the number of private-sector jobs increased 61,100, up 0.8 percent from September 2020. Florida’s unemployment rate decreased to 6.5 percent in October 2020. Florida’s labor force was down 14,000 (-0.1 percent) over the month.To view the October 2020 employment data, visit www.floridajobs.org/labor-market-information/labor-market-information-press-releases/monthly-press-releases.###Press Releases 2021"
