The company is privately funded by world-leading technology investors with strong commitment and support and our team is made of driven and talented people with a real passion for space innovation.We’re making rockets in a way that hasn’t been done before disrupting a traditional industry. If you are up for the challenge, want to work on cutting-edge projects and be part of a team changing the world for better, come, join us and launch your career!WHY JOIN USBe at the centre of an agile and dynamic team of highly motivated space enthusiastsAs one of the first employees, you will be part of an incredible start-up journeyHuge learning potential and the opportunity to build things from the ground up (literally)You’ll enjoy a high level of freedom and autonomyFlat hierarchy and open communicationTHE MISSIONThe main purpose would be to participate in the entire design cycle of the engine system and design structural components including conceptual and detailed design, thermo-fluid modelling and structural analysis. Optimize for low-cost production and take part in the planning and coordination of structure manufacturing and testing.More specifically, you will:Take over the design of engine structures necessary for the engine integrationDefine and allocate the mechanical interfaces between engine subsystemsUse ANSYS Mechanical to perform structural and thermal analyses (static, transient, modal) to develop a design concept and to iterate towards detailed designIntegration of components on to rocket engine top assemblyDevelop 3-D models, assembly & detail drawingsCoordination of manufacturing process and interface with vendor or in-house fabricationRequirementsWHO YOU ARE AND WHAT YOU HAVEMusts:Master’s degree or higher in an Aerospace Engineering, Mechanical Engineering or similarStrong background and demonstrated knowledge in solid mechanics, structural dynamics and thermal structural analysisKnowledge in the use of FEA analysis tools (ANSYS preferred)Ability to do hand calculations to design estimations and sanity-checksStrong practical experience with SolidWorks or similar CAD softwareKnowledge of fabrication and quality control relative to various manufacturing processesKnowledge of at least one programming/scripting language (Python/MATLAB/etc)Passionate about new space, curious and eager to learnDriven to deliver and constantly push the boundariesTake ownership and accountability for your own actions and mistakesHumble and willing to go our of your way to help othersCan-do attitude and outstanding communication skillsFluent English. German is highly preferredNice-to-haves:Experience in working with complex space systems (e.g. rocket engines, satellites, etc.)Note: Due to high volume of applications, we cannot get back to all candidates. If you have not heard from us within 2-4 weeks, please consider that your application has been unsuccessful for the current openings.
