Some examples are:QualcommSAIC (Science Applications International Corporation)IntuitBAE SystemsUniversity of California at San DiegoClick here for a full listing of companies in San Diego.More job boardsMany job seekers find success by posting their resumes on online job boards such as Monster, Dice, or Careerbuilder. This is because recruiters and employers can find you. Be aware that the same job may be posted on multiple job boards, and that job boards may contain out-of-date listings. For added safety, create and use a separate e-mail address for public visibility on job boards.In most cases, resumes can be posted anonymously to protect name, address, and other contact information, as well as current employment information. Once a resume is posted, companies who pay a fee to the job boards are permitted to perform a number of different types of searches to identify potential employees who meet their criteria.All job seekers are permitted to perform free searches for open positions on these boards. Registered users of some of these sites are permitted to sign up for and configure custom search agents, to deliver information about open positions meeting their criteria via e-mail.Posting a complete resume can be a lengthy process, but most sites employ wizard-type functionality to guide users through each of the required steps.The following table lists many of the top on-line job sites, but is not exhaustive.
