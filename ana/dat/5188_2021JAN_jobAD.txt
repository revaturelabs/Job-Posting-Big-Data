 Administrative Coordinator - Hurvitz Brain Sciences - Regular Full-Time (193182) 
 Sunnybrook Health Sciences Centre 
 __Toronto, ON 
 Apply Now 
 We currently have a Regular Full-Time opportunity for an Administrative Coordinator in the Hurvitz Brain Science Program at our Bayview Campus. 
 This is a new role created in partnership between Brain Sciences and the Department of Family and Community Medicine. The coordinator will provide administrative support to the Rapid Access to Addictions Medicine clinics, as well as the office of the Chief of the Department of Family and Community Medicine. 
 RAAM Clinic 
 A rapid access addictions medicine (RAAM) clinic is a low-barrier, walk-in clinic that patients can attend to get help for a substance use disorder without an appointment or formal referral. RAAM clinics provide time-limited medical addiction care (including pharmacotherapy, brief counselling, and referrals to community services). The administrative assistant is an integral member of the team, working collaboratively with physicians, nurse practitioners, counsellors, and peer support workers. The position is well suited to a self-starter with strong organizational and communication skills who enjoys being part of building new services, building partnerships internally and externally and working with diverse communities 
 The successful candidate will be responsible for: 
Scheduling and coordinating clinics for multiple physicians/ residents/ fellows/allied health using Outlook, scheduling software and patient registration system 
Greeting patients, answering patient queries and managing waiting room during clinic hours 
Responding to phone, e-mail and fax inquiries, and other medical related administrative duties as assigned 
Supporting outbound referrals as needed 
Collecting and reporting clinic data (visits, referral source, etc) 
Direct communication with patient’s primary care providers on an as-needed basis. 
Prioritizing urgent tasks and requests from physicians 
Coordinating and providing support for program development activities including scheduling meetings, coordinating materials, collection of data 
 Department of Family and Community Medicine 
 The successful candidate will be responsible for: 
Prepare meeting material, supporting documentation, prepare/assist with presentations 
Prepare meeting agendas, minutes, prepare/revise documents, prepare correspondence 
Preparation and submission of regularly scheduled reports (eg. CEO reports) 
Responsible for coordinating credentialing of DFCM physicians and coordinating academic appointments at the University of Toronto. 
Update and maintain departmental policies and procedures 
Administration of departmental invoices; data entry, log and deliver requests to Accounts Payable for processing, prepare/generate financial/cost centre reports 
Manage calendar, schedule meetings, coordinate arrangements/correspondence, room reservations, catering, equipment as required etc. 
Order and procurement of departmental supplies and equipment 
Open and distribute mail 
 Qualifications/Skills: 
Post-secondary degree or diploma in Office Administration, or recognized equivalent, or a combination of education and experience 
Minimum of 3 years in administrative assistant role (academic or community hospital experience is preferred) 
Excellent organizational, time management, and critical thinking skills 
Demonstrate initiative by identifying issues or areas for improvement with resolution 
Ability to communicate effectively with all levels of staff and maintain good relationships with administrative leaders, physicians, patients, hospital staff, stakeholders, external clients 
Proven ability to multi-task, set priorities, problem solve, work independently and with various team members 
Demonstrate advanced working knowledge of Microsoft Outlook, Word, Excel 
Demonstrate ability to take initiative and act independently using sound judgment as well as accepting direction 
Demonstrated experience in dealing with the public and patients in person, by telephone and e-mail in a courteous, pleasant and professional manner. 
Tact and discretion in dealing with sensitive and confidential issues 
Desire and ability to take on new and added responsibilities 
Ability to maintain strict confidentiality 
Knowledge of medical terminology is helpful
